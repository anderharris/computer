Medical Software

Medical software has been in use since the 1960s. These days there are a huge range of types of medical software from monitoring and diagnostic applications to as well as hospital manament tools.
The developement of medical software is kept consistent by the use of the benchmark standard IEC 62304. This is used by both the US and the EU as well as a number of other countries. Medical Imaging

There are many different methods of creating medical images, all of which rely on technology that would not exist without the advances in the field of Computer Science.

MRI scans produce 3D images that are very detailed. They are used for disease detection, diagnosis, and treatment monitoring. It is a non-invasive process that uses strong magnets which produce a magnetic field.

An X-Ray beam is sent through the body and is able to pass through body tissue but is blocked by bone. A receiver on the other side picks up the x-rays that have passed through the body and is able to build up an image of where the bones are based on this.

PET scanners detect radiation given off by a substance (often called a radiotracer) that is injected into your body. This allows it to see where the tracer collects in the body. PET scans are often used to see how well different parts of the body are working.

CAT (or CT) scanners produce images by combining a number of x-ray images taken at different angles. This produces a far more detailed image than a simple x-ray does.
